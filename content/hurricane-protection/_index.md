# Florida Hurricane Protection Cost Guides

Hurricane protection isn't optional in Florida — it's the difference between keeping your homeowners insurance and losing it. Impact windows, hurricane shutters, reinforced garage doors, and whole-house generators all factor into your wind mitigation rating, which directly affects your premium. In some cases, the insurance savings alone pay for the upgrade within 5-7 years.

Our hurricane protection guides cover every major Florida market with real installed pricing. Fort Lauderdale and Miami fall inside the HVHZ, which means every product needs a Miami-Dade NOA — that's a different price point than Tampa or Orlando, where standard Florida Product Approvals are sufficient. We break down those differences so you're not comparing costs that don't apply to your location.

Each guide includes the My Safe Florida Home program details, county permit processes, contractor licensing requirements, and the specific wind mitigation discounts you can expect from upgrading. Browse the guides for your area below.
