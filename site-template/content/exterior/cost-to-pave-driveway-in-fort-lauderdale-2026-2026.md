# How Much Does It Cost to Pave Driveway in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A new paved driveway in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$4,000 and $25,000** for most standard residential projects, with the typical homeowner paying around **$9,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are driveway size, material choice, and the complex drainage requirements dictated by local flood zones.

Fort Lauderdale sits on sandy soil with a high water table, which means a simple pour is rarely enough. You need proper grading and drainage to prevent pooling water that damages the slab. This adds cost but saves your foundation from long-term water issues.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$4,000 – $6,500** | 1-car asphalt, minimal prep |
| Mid-Range | **$6,500 – $12,000** | 2-car concrete, proper drainage |
| High-End | **$12,000 – $25,000+** | Pavers, 3-car, complex grading |

**Budget** covers a single-car asphalt patch or new strip on an existing stable base. It meets minimum code but lacks enhanced drainage or decorative finishes.

**Mid-range** is the standard choice for Fort Lauderdale — a full two-car concrete slab with proper sub-base grading and French drains. Includes permits and city inspections.

**High-end** involves stamped concrete, interlocking pavers, or a three-car layout with extensive excavation and retention pond integration. Often required for large lots near canals.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, site prep, disposal, standard permits, and cleanup. They do not include underground utility relocation or major foundation repairs discovered during excavation.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Driveway Size

Driveway pricing is usually calculated per square foot. A standard Fort Lauderdale home has a driveway between 500 and 1,000 sq ft. At **$8–$20 per sq ft** installed depending on material, a 500 sq ft asphalt driveway runs roughly **$4,000–$6,000** while a 1,000 sq ft concrete pad pushes **$8,000–$14,000**.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Asphalt is the most affordable option at **$4.00–$8.00 per sq ft** installed. Concrete — popular for durability in South Florida — jumps to **$8.00–$14.00**. Interlocking pavers are the premium choice at **$12.00–$25.00 per sq ft** but last significantly longer.

### 3. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Paving labor in Broward County runs **$45–$70 per hour**, roughly 15–20% above the national average. Demand stays high year-round from flood damage repairs and new construction. After heavy rains or tropical storms, rates can spike another 10–20% due to backlog.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

The City of Fort Lauderdale requires a building permit for any new driveway construction or replacement exceeding 200 square feet. Permit fees run **$150–$400** depending on project valuation. The process includes a zoning review and final inspection. Your contractor should pull the permit — if they suggest skipping it, walk away.

### 5. Site Preparation and Drainage

Fort Lauderdale soil is mostly sand with a high water table. You need a compacted sub-base of crushed stone to prevent sinking. Adding French drains or catch basins adds **$1,500–$4,000** to the project but prevents flooding during heavy rains.

### 6. Flood Zone Requirements

Many Fort Lauderdale properties lie in FEMA flood zones AE or VE. You may need to elevate the driveway elevation to match flood plain requirements. This often requires adding fill dirt, which increases hauling costs by **$2,000–$5,000**.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Asphalt | **$4.00 – $8.00** | 15–20 years |
| Concrete (Plain) | **$8.00 – $12.00** | 25–30 years |
| Concrete (Stamped) | **$12.00 – $18.00** | 25–35 years |
| Pavers | **$15.00 – $25.00** | 30–50 years |
| Gravel | **$2.00 – $4.00** | 5–10 years |

**Asphalt** is the budget option. It requires sealing every 3–5 years to prevent cracking from UV exposure. Best for rental properties or budget-conscious homeowners.

**Concrete** hits the sweet spot — rated for heavy loads, 30-year lifespan, and the best cost-to-value ratio. This is what most Fort Lauderdale homeowners pick for durability.

**Stamped concrete** mimics stone or brick patterns but costs roughly 50% more than plain concrete. Your contractor adds texture while the pour is fresh.

**Pavers** are premium — 50+ year lifespan, 180 mph wind resistance, and the aesthetic that fits high-end Fort Lauderdale neighborhoods. Expect to pay **$15.00–$25.00 per sq ft**.

**Gravel** offers the cheapest upfront cost but requires constant maintenance to prevent ruts and weed growth. Best for long rural driveways or temporary solutions.

**Driveway gates** often accompany new paving. Installing an automatic gate system adds **$3,000–$8,000** to the total exterior project scope.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** The Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all paving work in Fort Lauderdale. Key requirements include minimum driveway width of 12 feet for two cars, proper slope for drainage, and erosion control measures during construction. These go beyond what most states require.

**Flood Zones:** Fort Lauderdale is prone to coastal flooding. Many homes are in high-risk flood zones. If you are building near a canal or waterway, you may need a separate permit from the South Florida Water Management District. This adds **$500–$1,500** in fees and time.

**Permits:** Apply through the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Division online portal or at City Hall. Turnaround is 10–15 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$150–$400** for the fee. One final inspection required.

**Insurance Impact:** A new driveway does not directly lower premiums like a new roof. However, proper drainage prevents water intrusion into the home. Some insurers offer discounts for flood mitigation improvements that protect the structure from foundation water damage.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities restrict driveway width, material, and color. Concrete is often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement — changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

**Season:** Best time to pave here is December through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm delays. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay concrete curing.

**Soil Conditions:** South Florida soil is highly porous sand. You need at least 6 inches of compacted crushed stone base to support the slab. Without this, the driveway will sink within 3–5 years due to rain saturation.

## How to Save Money on Driveway Paving in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$9,000** job, that's **$1,800–$3,000** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, tear-off, permits, and disposal separately.

2. **Schedule in dry season.** December through April is slower for pavers. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

3. **Choose asphalt over pavers if your HOA allows it.** The savings between asphalt (**$4.00–$8.00/sq ft**) and pavers (**$15.00–$25.00/sq ft**) can be **$8,000–$15,000** on a typical two-car driveway.

4. **Bundle drainage upgrades with the driveway.** Adding catch basins or French drains during a replacement is **40–60% cheaper** than standalone projects. The combined drainage improvements also prevent future foundation water damage.

5. **Check for permit fee waivers.** Sometimes small repair projects under a certain dollar amount qualify for reduced fees. Ask your contractor if the project qualifies for a minor repair permit instead of a full new construction permit.

## When to Replace Your Driveway — Warning Signs

**Your driveway is 15–20 years old.** Asphalt in South Florida degrades faster than in cooler climates due to intense UV, heat cycling, and salt air. A 20-year-old driveway in Fort Lauderdale has taken more abuse than a 30-year-old driveway in the Midwest.

**Water pools in cracks.** If water sits in potholes or cracks after rain, the base is failing. This is common in flood zones where the water table rises. It leads to rapid deterioration of the surface layer.

**Visible cracks wider than ¼ inch.** Hairline cracks can be sealed. Cracks wider than a quarter inch indicate structural failure of the sub-base. Sealing won't fix the underlying shift.

**Settling or sinking slabs.** If you can feel a drop-off where the driveway meets the house or garage, the soil has compacted. This requires excavation and re-pouring, not just resurfacing.

## How to Hire a Driveway Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC) or a Certified Pool and Spa Contractor if working near pools.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Fort Lauderdale local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, sub-base type, number of squares, tear-off, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, sub-base complete, pour complete, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a driveway paving cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$4,000 and $25,000**. The average is around **$9,500**. Your actual cost depends on driveway size, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to pave my driveway in Fort Lauderdale?

Yes, always for new construction or replacement. Apply through the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Division. Budget **$150–$400**. One inspection required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a driveway paving project take in Fort Lauderdale?

Asphalt driveways take **2–3 days** for an average home. Concrete driveways take **5–7 days** due to curing time. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays.

### Does a new driveway increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A new driveway recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is drainage — a home with proper drainage is dramatically easier to sell because buyers know the foundation is safe from water intrusion.

### Can I pave my own driveway in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all drainage requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed driveway. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover driveway replacement?

Storm damage (wind, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.
