# How Much Does It Cost to Pressure Wash in Orlando? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A standard exterior pressure washing job in Orlando costs between **$300 and $900** for most single-family homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$550**. The biggest factors driving your price are the square footage of the surface, the level of mold or algae growth, and the specific cleaning method required.

Orlando humidity creates a unique cleaning challenge compared to drier climates. The constant moisture allows green algae, black streaks, and moss to take hold on roofs, driveways, and siding much faster than in other parts of the country.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$300 – $450** | Driveway or small patio |
| Mid-Range | **$450 – $700** | Full driveway + walkway |
| High-End | **$700 – $950+** | Full exterior + roof soft wash |

**Budget** covers high-pressure cleaning of a single concrete surface like a driveway or a small patio area. It usually does not include the house siding or roof. This is a quick job, often finished in 2 to 3 hours.

**Mid-range** is the most common choice for Orlando homeowners. It includes the driveway, walkways, and side yard patios. It involves pre-treatment for algae and a rinse-down. Most 2,000 sq ft homes fall into this bracket.

**High-end** includes soft washing the roof and washing the exterior siding. This requires specialized low-pressure equipment and biodegradable cleaning solutions. It takes longer and requires more labor to avoid damaging stucco or shingles.

These prices reflect Orlando market rates as of early 2026. They include labor, equipment, cleaning solutions, and water. They do not include sealing the concrete or repairs to cracked pavers found during the wash.

## What Affects the Cost in Orlando

### 1. Surface Area

Pressure washing is often priced by square footage or by linear footage for driveways. A standard two-car driveway is about 600 square feet. Cleaning just the driveway costs **$150–$250**. Washing the entire perimeter of a home, including all patios and walkways, adds **$200–$400** to the total.

### 2. Surface Material

Concrete is the easiest surface to clean and costs the least. Stucco requires low-pressure soft washing to avoid damaging the finish. Cleaning stucco adds **$200–$400** compared to concrete because it takes longer and requires more care. Wood decks are the most delicate and often cost **$0.50–$1.00 per sq ft**.

### 3. Contamination Level

Orlando homes sit in a humid environment. If your siding has heavy black algae streaks or your driveway has oil stains, the price goes up. Heavy contamination requires pre-soaking with chemicals and multiple passes. This adds **$100–$200** to the labor time and chemical costs.

### 4. Accessibility

If you need to wash the second story of a home, the price increases. Ground-level cleaning is standard. Second-story siding requires ladders, scaffolding, or lift equipment. Adding second-story access adds **$150–$300** to the project.

### 5. Water Source

Contractors bring their own water supply in a truck for larger jobs. If you provide water from your home spigot, they might charge less. However, water usage charges usually apply to the contractor regardless. The difference is usually small, around **$50–$75** savings if you provide water.

### 6. Chemical Treatment

Standard pressure washing uses water pressure alone. Soft washing uses chemicals to kill organic growth. Chemicals are necessary for roofs and stucco. Chemical costs range from **$50 to $150** depending on the size of the property and the strength of the solution needed.

## Cost by Material Type

| Surface | Cost per Sq Ft | Total Estimate (Avg Home) |
|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Concrete Driveway | **$0.20 – $0.40** | **$150 – $300** |
| Walkways & Pavers | **$0.30 – $0.60** | **$100 – $250** |
| House Siding (Stucco) | **$0.40 – $0.80** | **$400 – $800** |
| Roof (Soft Wash) | **$0.50 – $1.00** | **$500 – $1,200** |
| Wood Deck | **$0.50 – $1.00** | **$300 – $600** |

**Concrete driveways** are the most common service. They resist high pressure well. However, old concrete in Orlando often has cracks where weeds grow. Cleaning them reveals the age of the slab. Expect to spend more if sealing is added after the wash.

**Walkways and pavers** cost more per square foot than driveways. The grout lines hold dirt and algae. High-pressure water can dislodge sand from between pavers. Soft washing is often preferred to preserve the joint sand.

**House siding** requires the most caution. Most Orlando homes have stucco or vinyl. Stucco absorbs water. High pressure can force water behind the stucco and cause mold inside the walls. Low pressure soft washing is safer and slightly more expensive.

**Roof cleaning** is strictly soft washing. High pressure will strip the granules off asphalt shingles. It also voids most roof warranties. Roof cleaning removes black streaks caused by Gloeocapsa magma algae. This is a specialized service.

**Wood decks** need careful handling. High pressure splinters wood. A dedicated deck cleaner is needed to lift the gray weathering. If the wood is rotting, washing might reveal damage that needs repair. Always inspect the wood before washing.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Orlando does not require a building permit for standard residential pressure washing. However, Orange County has strict stormwater management rules. You cannot wash toxic chemicals into the storm drain. Contractors must use biodegradable detergents and contain runoff if possible.

**HVHZ:** Orlando is not in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone. This means wind mitigation codes are less strict than in Miami. However, wind speeds still reach 140 mph. Contractors must be insured for wind damage while on ladders. Verify their liability coverage before hiring.

**Permits:** While no building permit is needed, commercial contractors need an Orange County Business Tax Receipt. Homeowners doing it themselves do not need a permit. However, if you are cleaning a rental property, the city may require a rental registration inspection.

**Insurance Impact:** Pressure washing can prevent costly repairs. Removing algae from your roof extends the life of shingles. Cleaning gutters prevents water damage to the foundation. A well-maintained home may see lower claims from mold or water intrusion over time.

**HOA:** Many Orlando communities have deed restrictions on exterior appearance. Some HOAs require pre-approval for pressure washing. They may specify the color of cleaning chemicals or the time of day you can work. Check your HOA rules to avoid fines.

**Season:** Best time to wash is late fall through early spring. Summer in Orlando brings daily thunderstorms. Rain can ruin the cleaning immediately after you finish. Avoid June through September due to humidity and rain delays.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program offers grants for home inspections and hardening. While pressure washing is not a hardening improvement, a clean home inspection is part of maintenance. Use the inspection results to identify if washing is needed before selling.

## How to Save Money on Pressure Washing in Orlando

1.  **Get at least 3 written quotes.** Pricing varies by 30% between contractors in Orlando. A driveway wash might be **$180** at one place and **$260** at another. Ensure each quote includes chemicals, water, and cleanup.

2.  **Schedule in off-season.** Winter and early spring are slower for exterior cleaning. You will have more availability. Some contractors offer **10–15%** discounts in January and February to keep crews busy.

3.  **Bundle services together.** Cleaning your driveway, walkways, and patio in one visit is cheaper than three separate trips. Labor travel time is included in one charge. Ask for a package deal on all exterior hard surfaces.

4.  **Clear the area yourself.** Move cars, potted plants, and patio furniture before the crew arrives. This saves them time. Some contractors charge **$50–$100** for labor to move heavy items. Clearing it yourself keeps the price down.

5.  **Check for seasonal promotions.** Many local companies run sales during home improvement months. Look for "Spring Clean" or "Summer Sale" promotions. These often include free gutter rinses with a full exterior wash.

## When to Pressure Wash Your Home — Warning Signs

**You see green or black streaks on the roof.** These are algae blooms common in Florida. They eat into the shingle granules. If left alone, they can shorten the roof life by 5 years or more. Wash it before it causes damage.

**Mold or mildew on the siding.** Dark spots on stucco or vinyl are mold spores. They thrive in Orlando's humidity. Cleaning them prevents the spores from entering cracks and causing interior mold. This is a health issue for residents.

**Slippery walkways.** Moss and algae make concrete and pavers slick. This is a liability risk for guests. If someone slips on a dirty walkway, you could be liable. Clean these areas regularly to ensure safety.

**Dirt buildup on the driveway.** Oil stains and tire marks accumulate over time. They look bad and attract pests. A deep clean removes the top layer of dirt and restores the concrete look. This improves curb appeal for resale.

## How to Hire a Pressure Washing Contractor in Orlando

1.  **Verify their license.** Look for a Certified Home Improvement Contractor or similar credential. You can check licenses at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). Ensure the license is active and in good standing.

2.  **Confirm liability insurance.** Ask for a certificate showing at least **$1 million** in general liability. Pressure washing can damage property. If they hit a window or damage a pipe, you need coverage.

3.  **Check for a local business tax receipt.** Orlando contractors must have a valid business tax receipt to operate legally. This proves they are registered locally, not a transient crew.

4.  **Ask about chemicals.** Verify they use biodegradable, eco-friendly solutions. Do not let them use harsh acids that could kill your lawn. Ask for the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) of the products they use.

5.  **Get a written contract.** The contract should list the surfaces to be cleaned, the method (pressure or soft wash), the price, and the timeline. Avoid verbal agreements entirely.

6.  **Check references.** Ask for 2–3 recent client references in Orlando. Call them to ask about punctuality and cleanup. Look for complaints about water damage or missed spots.

7.  **Never pay 100% upfront.** Pay a deposit to secure the date, and the rest upon completion. Florida law protects against large upfront deposits. Paying at the end ensures you get the service you paid for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does pressure washing cost in Orlando in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$300 and $900**. The average is around **$550**. Your actual cost depends on the size of the property and how dirty the surfaces are.

### Do I need a permit to pressure wash my home in Orlando?

No, a building permit is not required for residential cleaning. However, you must follow Orange County stormwater regulations. Do not wash toxic chemicals into the storm drain.

### How long does a pressure washing job take?

A driveway takes **1–2 hours**. A full exterior wash takes **3–5 hours**. The time depends on the size of the house and how many passes are needed to remove the algae.

### Can pressure washing damage my roof?

Yes, high pressure can strip asphalt shingles. You must use soft washing for roofs. If a contractor uses high pressure on your roof, it voids your warranty.

### How often should I pressure wash my house?

Clean your driveway once a year. Wash your roof every 2–3 years. Wash the siding every 2 years to prevent mold buildup. Florida humidity requires more frequent cleaning than other states.

### Is pressure washing safe for my plants?

High pressure can damage leaves. Runoff from chemicals can kill grass. Cover plants before washing or use a neutralizing solution. Ask your contractor to rinse plants after the job.
