# How Much Does It Cost to Paint House Exterior in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full exterior paint job in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$4,500 and $14,000** for most single-family homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$8,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are surface preparation, siding type, and Broward County's strict humidity and mold-resistant coating requirements.

Fort Lauderdale's coastal climate makes painting more than just a cosmetic upgrade. Salt air, intense UV rays, and high humidity degrade paint faster here than in most of the country. That means you need higher-grade materials and more prep work to ensure the finish lasts.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$4,500 – $7,000** | 1,200 sq ft, 1 coat, basic prep |
| Mid-Range | **$7,000 – $11,000** | 2,000 sq ft, 2 coats, full prep |
| High-End | **$11,000 – $14,000+** | 2,500+ sq ft, historic, premium paint |

**Budget** gets you a one-coat job on a small home with minimal repair. It often skips pressure washing or caulking. It meets basic code but won't last as long in Florida's weather.

**Mid-range** is what most homeowners choose. It includes full pressure washing, scraping loose paint, caulking all seams, and applying two coats of premium 100% acrylic latex. This covers standard siding types like stucco or wood.

**High-end** covers larger estates, historic homes, or those with extensive damage. Includes specialized primers for mold resistance, detailed caulking, and top-tier brands like Sherwin Williams or Benjamin Moore.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, scaffolding, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs if rot is found behind the siding during prep.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Surface Preparation

This is the single biggest cost driver. In Fort Lauderdale, you cannot just paint over old peeling paint. You must scrape, sand, and prime. Proper prep adds **$1.50–$3.00 per sq ft** to the total project. Skipping prep saves money now but leads to peeling within two years.

### 2. Siding Material

Stucco is the most common siding in Fort Lauderdale neighborhoods built from 1940 to 1980. It absorbs moisture and requires specific breathable primers. Wood siding needs more frequent maintenance and costs **$1.00–$2.00 more per sq ft** due to delicate scraping. Brick and concrete block are easier to paint but require etching.

### 3. Labor Rates in Broward County

Painting labor in Fort Lauderdale runs **$45–$75 per hour**, roughly 15% above the national average. Skilled crews who know how to handle humidity and salt air command higher rates. Demand spikes in spring before hurricane season, pushing prices up another **5–10%**.

### 4. Paint Quality

Cheap exterior paint lasts 3–5 years in Florida. Premium paint with mold inhibitors lasts 10–15 years. The material cost difference is **$2.00–$5.00 per gallon**. A typical house needs 10–20 gallons of paint. Spending extra on paint now saves money on labor later.

### 5. Accessibility and Scaffolding

Many Fort Lauderdale homes are two stories high. You cannot paint them with a ladder safely. Scaffolding or swing stages add **$1,000–$3,000** to the project. If the house has a second-story balcony, painters must work around railings, which slows down the process.

### 6. HOA Approval

Most Fort Lauderdale communities have architectural review committees. You must submit paint colors and samples before work starts. Changing colors mid-job is not allowed and can result in fines. Get written approval before signing a contractor agreement to avoid delays.

## Cost by Siding Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Prep Needed |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Stucco | **$2.50 – $4.50** | Crack filling, priming |
| Wood Siding | **$3.00 – $5.00** | Scraping, sanding, sealing |
| Brick | **$2.00 – $3.50** | Etching, cleaning |
| Concrete Block | **$2.00 – $3.50** | Etching, priming |
| Vinyl | **$2.50 – $4.00** | Cleaning, no scraping |
| Hardie Board | **$2.50 – $4.50** | Caulking seams, priming |

**Stucco** dominates Fort Lauderdale's housing stock. It cracks easily due to settling. You must fill hairline cracks with elastomeric caulk before painting. If you skip this, moisture gets behind the paint and causes bubbling.

**Wood siding** requires the most care. Old wood rots fast in Florida's humidity. Every loose board needs replacement before painting. Sealers are mandatory to prevent water intrusion behind the wood.

**Brick** is low maintenance but difficult to clean. High-pressure washing removes decades of salt buildup. You cannot use too much pressure or you damage the mortar. Specialized cleaners cost extra.

**Concrete block** is common in newer construction. It needs etching to remove surface laitance. A high-quality primer is required to prevent alkali burn from the concrete.

**Vinyl** cannot be painted easily. It expands and contracts with heat. If you paint it, you must use vinyl-safe paint. Many contractors refuse this job because they cannot guarantee the finish.

**Hardie Board** is fiber cement. It holds paint well but needs proper caulking at joints. If water gets behind the boards, it rots the wood framing underneath.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) requires all exterior coatings to be mold and mildew resistant. This is not optional in South Florida. Look for paint with EPA registration for antimicrobial protection. Standard paint will grow black spots within a year.

**Humidity:** Fort Lauderdale averages **75% humidity** year-round. Paint needs to breathe to let moisture escape the walls. Oil-based paints trap moisture and blister. Use 100% acrylic latex paint for all exterior applications.

**Salt Air:** Homes near the Intracoastal or ocean face salt spray. Salt corrodes metal trim and degrades paint binders. Use rust-inhibitive primers on all metal components. Stainless steel screws are required for trim to prevent staining.

**Permits:** Cosmetic painting does not require a building permit from the City of Fort Lauderdale. However, if you are replacing siding or repairing structural rot, a permit is mandatory. The permit fee runs **$200–$500** for structural repairs.

**Insurance Impact:** Mold is the leading cause of insurance claims in Florida. A fresh coat of mold-resistant paint can prevent water intrusion claims. Some carriers offer discounts for homes with newer exteriors because they indicate good maintenance.

**HOA:** Broward County HOAs are strict. They often restrict dark colors that absorb heat. You must submit a color palette for approval. The review process takes **10–15 business days**. Start this process before getting quotes.

**Season:** Best time to paint is January through May. Humidity is lower, and rain is less frequent. Avoid hurricane season (June through November). Rain delays projects, and high humidity prevents paint from curing properly.

**My Safe Florida Home:** While painting isn't a direct grant item, the program offers free wind inspections. During these inspections, they check for moisture intrusion. Fixing these issues before painting ensures the new paint lasts longer.

## How to Save Money on Exterior Painting in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$8,000** job, that's **$1,600–$2,800** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes prep, priming, and top coats separately.

2.  **Schedule in dry season.** January through May is slower for painters. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

3.  **Do your own prep work.** If you can pressure wash the house or clear furniture from the perimeter, contractors may lower the labor cost by **$500–$1,000**. Just verify they are okay with this before signing.

4.  **Choose premium paint over labor savings.** Paint is cheaper than labor. Spending **$500** more on premium paint now saves you **$3,000** in repainting costs in 5 years. Don't let a contractor upsell you on cheap paint.

5.  **Bundle with other exterior work.** If you need gutter cleaning or window washing, bundle it with the paint job. Contractors often discount bundled services by **10–15%**. This saves you on mobilization fees.

## When to Paint Your House — Warning Signs

**Your paint is peeling or chalking.** Run your hand over the wall. If it feels rough or leaves white dust, the binder has failed. This usually happens 10–15 years after the last coat.

**Mold or mildew spots appear.** Black or green spots indicate moisture intrusion. In Fort Lauderdale, this can hide rot behind the siding. Do not paint over mold. It must be killed and cleaned first.

**Cracks in stucco.** Hairline cracks are normal, but wide cracks indicate structural settling. Fill them before painting. If you don't, the paint will crack right over the gap.

**Rust on metal trim.** Check your gutters, downspouts, and window frames. Rust stains bleed through new paint. Sand them down and prime with a rust-inhibitive primer.

**Faded colors.** UV rays fade color faster in Florida. If the house looks dull, a fresh coat restores curb appeal. This is also a good time to check for other exterior damage.

## How to Hire a Painting Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3.  **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, number of coats, prep work, timeline, warranty, and cleanup.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6.  **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, prep complete, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, paint specs with VOC compliance, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to paint the exterior of a house in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$4,500 and $14,000**. The average is around **$8,500**. Your actual cost depends on house size, siding condition, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to paint my house in Broward County?

No, cosmetic painting does not require a building permit. However, if you are repairing structural rot or replacing siding, a permit is mandatory. The fee runs **$200–$500**.

### How long does an exterior paint job take in Fort Lauderdale?

A standard single-family home takes **3–7 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays. Scaffolding setup adds an extra day.

### Does painting increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A fresh coat of paint recoups **50–60%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is curb appeal. A well-maintained exterior signals to buyers that the home is cared for.

### Can I paint my own house exterior in Florida?

Yes, if you own and occupy the home. But you must meet all VOC regulations and safety standards. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed roof if water damage occurs. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover exterior paint?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered. Normal wear and fading is never covered. If a storm blows off a section of siding, the repair is covered.
