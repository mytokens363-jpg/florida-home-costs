# How Much Does It Cost to Remodel a Kitchen in Orlando? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full kitchen remodel in Orlando costs between **$20,000 and $100,000** for most homeowners, with the average project landing around **$45,000**. The biggest factors driving your price are cabinet quality, countertop material, and whether you are moving plumbing or electrical lines.

Orlando construction costs have risen with material inflation and labor shortages. A simple refresh stays under **$30,000**, but a luxury renovation with custom cabinetry and high-end appliances pushes well past **$75,000**. You are paying for local expertise in Florida Building Code compliance and hurricane-resistant fixtures.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$20,000 – $30,000** | Paint, new hardware, minor updates |
| Mid-Range | **$35,000 – $55,000** | New cabinets, counters, flooring |
| High-End | **$60,000 – $100,000+** | Custom cabinetry, luxury appliances |

**Budget** covers cosmetic updates that do not change the layout. You are keeping existing plumbing and electrical rough-ins. This scope includes refacing cabinets, installing vinyl or tile flooring, and updating lighting fixtures.

**Mid-range** is the sweet spot for most Orlando homeowners. It involves replacing all cabinets with semi-custom options, installing quartz or granite countertops, and updating the sink and faucet. Includes new flooring and standard appliance package.

**High-end** means custom work. Full layout changes, moving walls, moving gas lines, and installing top-tier appliances like Sub-Zero or Wolf. Includes premium quartzite or marble countertops and custom lighting design.

These prices reflect Orlando market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, disposal, and standard permits. They do not include structural engineering fees if a load-bearing wall is removed.

## What Affects the Cost in Orlando

### 1. Kitchen Size and Layout

Kitchen size drives material quantities directly. A standard Orlando kitchen is **100 to 150 square feet**. Changing the layout adds significant cost. Moving a sink or stove requires new plumbing and electrical lines, which adds **$2,000–$5,000** to the project.

### 2. Cabinet Quality

Cabinets are the single biggest expense in most kitchens. Stock cabinets run **$100–$150 per linear foot**. Semi-custom options cost **$150–$300 per linear foot**. Full custom cabinetry with soft-close hardware and premium wood starts at **$400+ per linear foot**.

### 3. Countertop Materials

Countertops vary wildly in price. Laminate is the budget choice at **$20–$40 per square foot**. Quartz is the current favorite in Florida due to heat resistance at **$50–$100 per square foot**. Natural stone like granite or marble ranges from **$60–$150 per square foot**.

### 4. Labor Rates in Orlando

Construction labor in Central Florida runs **$45–$75 per hour** for skilled trades. Specialized trades like plumbers and electricians charge **$80–$120 per hour**. Demand spikes during the dry season (November–April), pushing labor rates toward the higher end of the range.

### 5. Permits and Inspections

Orange County Building Services requires permits for any structural, plumbing, or electrical changes. Permit fees typically run **$500–$2,000** depending on the scope of work. You need separate permits for electrical, plumbing, and building. This process adds **2–4 weeks** to the timeline.

### 6. Appliance Selection

Standard appliances cost **$2,000–$5,000** for a full set. High-end integrated appliances can cost **$10,000–$20,000**. Built-in refrigerators and dishwashers require custom cabinetry integration, which adds **$1,000–$3,000** in carpentry costs.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft or Unit | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Laminate Countertops | **$20 – $40** | 10–15 years |
| Quartz Countertops | **$50 – $100** | 20+ years |
| Granite Countertops | **$60 – $150** | 30+ years |
| Vinyl Flooring | **$3 – $7** | 10–15 years |
| Tile Flooring | **$5 – $15** | 20–50 years |
| Hardwood Flooring | **$8 – $20** | 30+ years |

**Laminate countertops** are the budget option. They look decent but scratch easily. In Florida’s humidity, they can delaminate if water gets underneath. Best for rental properties or strict budgets.

**Quartz countertops** are the most popular choice in Orlando. They are non-porous, resistant to stains, and handle the heat well. Prices have stabilized in 2026, making them a better value than natural stone for most homeowners.

**Granite countertops** offer a natural stone look with high durability. Each slab is unique. They require sealing every 1–2 years to prevent staining. In Orlando, granite handles the sun better than some other stones.

**Vinyl flooring** is affordable and water-resistant. Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP) looks like wood but costs less. It is perfect for Orlando kitchens where spills happen often. Easy to install over existing subfloors.

**Tile flooring** is durable and easy to clean. Ceramic tile is budget-friendly at **$5–$8 per sq ft**. Porcelain tile is harder and more water-resistant at **$8–$15 per sq ft**. Grout requires sealing to prevent mold in humid weather.

**Hardwood flooring** adds warmth but is risky in kitchens. Water damage can warp the wood. If you choose hardwood, ensure you have a large sink and good ventilation. Expect to pay **$8–$20 per sq ft** installed.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all renovations in Orlando. Kitchen plumbing must use approved materials for potable water. Electrical outlets must be GFCI protected near sinks and wet areas. These requirements ensure safety during Florida’s heavy rain events.

**Hurricane Zone:** Orlando is not in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), but it is in a moderate wind zone. If you are replacing exterior doors that open into the kitchen, they must meet impact-resistant standards. Interior cabinetry does not require wind ratings.

**Permits:** Apply through Orange County Building Services online portal or in person at the Orange County Government Center. Fees are based on project valuation. Expect **$500–$2,000** for permit fees. Two inspections are required: rough-in and final.

**Insurance Impact:** Water damage is a major claim in Florida. Upgrading plumbing to PEX-A or copper can lower your risk of leaks. Some insurers offer discounts for updated plumbing systems. Keep all receipts and permits for insurance verification.

**Humidity and Mold:** Orlando’s humidity averages **70% year-round**. Ventilation is critical. Install a high-capacity range hood vented to the outside. This prevents mold growth in cabinets and prevents moisture damage to drywall.

**Season:** Best time to remodel is January through April. The weather is dry, and contractors have availability. Summer months bring rain delays and higher humidity, which slows down drying times for paint and adhesives.

**My Safe Florida Home:** While primarily for wind mitigation, homeowners may qualify for inspections that identify water intrusion risks. If your kitchen remodel involves replacing windows or exterior doors, check for available grants for impact-rated products.

## How to Save Money on Kitchen Remodels in Orlando

1. **Keep the plumbing layout.** Moving the sink or dishwasher costs thousands. Keep fixtures in their current locations to save **$3,000–$6,000** on plumbing and labor.

2. **Refinish instead of replace.** If cabinets are structurally sound, refacing them saves money. Refacing costs **$5,000–$10,000** compared to **$20,000+** for full replacement.

3. **Choose quartz over stone.** Quartz is durable and requires no sealing. It is often cheaper than granite in the long run due to maintenance savings. You save **$10–$20 per sq ft** upfront.

4. **Source your own appliances.** Contractors often mark up appliances. Buy from local Orlando appliance stores and coordinate delivery yourself. You can save **$1,000–$3,000** on the appliance package.

5. **Do the demo yourself.** If you have the time and tools, demoing the old kitchen saves labor costs. Contractors charge **$1,000–$3,000** for demo and haul-away. Check with your contractor first to ensure it doesn't void warranties.

## When to Remodel Your Kitchen — Warning Signs

**Your cabinets are loose or warped.** Humidity causes wood to expand and contract. Doors that no longer align or drawers that stick indicate moisture damage. This is a sign of potential mold inside the cabinet boxes.

**Your appliances are outdated.** Older dishwashers and refrigerators use more electricity and water. Upgrading to Energy Star models lowers utility bills. A 10-year-old fridge costs significantly more to run than a 2026 model.

**You have visible water stains.** Stains on ceilings or walls near the sink indicate a leak. In Florida, a slow leak leads to black mold within weeks. Do not paint over the stain; fix the plumbing first.

**Your kitchen feels cramped.** If you cannot open the fridge door or move around the island, the layout is inefficient. Remodeling allows you to reconfigure the flow. A proper workflow saves time and frustration daily.

## How to Hire a Kitchen Contractor in Orlando

1. **Verify their Florida license.** Search at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered General Contractor (CGC) or Remodeler license.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum **$1 million**) and workers comp. Call the insurer to verify the policy is active.

3. **Check for Orange County registration.** Ensure they have a local business tax receipt for Orange County. This confirms they are registered to work in the area, not a transient crew.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — materials, labor, permits, timeline, and warranty. Compare scope carefully, not just the bottom line.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints about cleanup or delays. One bad review is normal; five bad reviews is a red flag.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, rough-in, drywall, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with model numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and permit responsibility.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Orlando in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$35,000 and $55,000** for a mid-range remodel. Full luxury renovations exceed **$75,000**. Cosmetic updates stay under **$30,000**. Your actual cost depends on cabinets, counters, and layout changes.

### Do I need a permit to remodel my kitchen in Orange County?

Yes, for any plumbing, electrical, or structural changes. Apply through Orange County Building Services. Permit fees range from **$500 to $2,000**. Painting or replacing flooring usually does not require a permit.

### How long does a kitchen remodel take in Orlando?

A standard remodel takes **6–10 weeks**. This includes demolition, rough-ins, installation, and final inspections. Delays can occur due to permit approvals or material backorders. Summer rain can add 1–2 weeks.

### Does a kitchen remodel increase home value in Orlando?

A kitchen remodel recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. In Orlando’s competitive market, a modern kitchen is a key selling point. Buyers expect updated appliances and countertops in homes over **$300,000**.

### Can I do a partial kitchen remodel myself?

You can paint, replace hardware, or install flooring. You cannot do plumbing or electrical work without a license in Florida. DIY electrical can void your insurance policy if a fire occurs.

### Does homeowners insurance cover kitchen remodels?

Standard policies do not cover improvements. They cover sudden damage like a burst pipe. If you file a claim for water damage, the insurance pays for repairs, not upgrades. Keep all permits for future claims.
