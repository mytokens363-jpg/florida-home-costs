# How Much Does It Cost to Replace Kitchen Cabinets in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full kitchen cabinet replacement in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$5,000 and $45,000** for most homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$15,000**. The biggest factors driving your price are cabinet material, kitchen layout complexity, and Fort Lauderdale’s strict building permit requirements for plumbing and electrical changes.

Fort Lauderdale homes range from 1960s single-family homes to modern high-rise condos. Each has unique structural needs for cabinet installation. You cannot just rip out old boxes and put new ones in without checking for water damage or foundation shifts.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$5,000 – $10,000** | Stock boxes, laminate, simple layout |
| Mid-Range | **$10,000 – $20,000** | Semi-custom, soft-close, new counters |
| High-End | **$20,000 – $45,000+** | Full custom, solid wood, hardware |

**Budget** projects usually involve stock cabinets from big-box stores like Home Depot or Lowe’s. These are pre-manufactured sizes that fit standard spaces. Labor is often handled by general handymen or smaller crews rather than specialized kitchen remodelers.

**Mid-range** is what most Fort Lauderdale homeowners choose for their primary residence. This includes semi-custom cabinets with soft-close hinges, upgraded door styles, and often new quartz countertops. It covers a standard 10x10 kitchen layout with standard plumbing relocation.

**High-end** covers full custom cabinetry built on-site or ordered from specialty manufacturers. This includes solid wood, specialty finishes, and complex organizational hardware. It often requires structural changes to walls or moving plumbing lines significantly.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, disposal of old cabinets, and basic installation. They do not include structural wall removal, new flooring, or major appliance purchases unless specified.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Kitchen Layout and Size

Cabinet pricing is often calculated by linear foot or per box. A standard galley kitchen has about **$10,000–$15,000** in cabinets. A large U-shaped kitchen with an island pushes costs to **$20,000–$35,000**. Every inch of linear footage you add increases material and labor time.

### 2. Cabinet Material and Quality

The material is the biggest cost driver. Laminate and thermofoil are the cheapest options at **$100–$200 per linear foot**. Solid wood like oak or maple starts at **$300–$500 per linear foot**. Veneered cabinets sit in the middle, offering wood grain at a lower price point.

### 3. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Specialized kitchen installers in Broward County charge **$40–$75 per hour**. This is higher than general handymen because cabinet installation requires precision. A full cabinet swap takes 3–7 days depending on the scope. Labor costs can range from **$3,000 to $8,000** for a standard job.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Fort Lauderdale requires permits if you move plumbing or electrical. The City of Fort Lauderdale Building Department handles this. Permit fees run **$150–$400** for a standard remodel. You cannot legally move a sink or dishwasher without a plumbing permit.

### 5. Removal and Disposal

You must haul away old cabinets. Fort Lauderdale has specific waste disposal rules. Hauling fees add **$300–$800** to the project. If you have hazardous materials like old lead paint or asbestos in the cabinetry, disposal costs jump significantly.

### 6. Countertop and Sink Integration

Most cabinet replacements include new countertops. Quartz slabs cost **$60–$100 per sq ft** installed. Granite is cheaper at **$40–$80 per sq ft**. Undermount sinks and new faucets add another **$500–$1,500** to the total bill.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Linear Foot | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Laminate | **$100 – $200** | 10–15 years |
| Thermofoil | **$150 – $300** | 10–15 years |
| MDF Painted | **$200 – $400** | 15–20 years |
| Plywood Box | **$300 – $500** | 20–30 years |
| Solid Wood | **$500 – $900** | 30+ years |
| Custom Built | **$800 – $1,500** | 40+ years |

**Laminate** is the most affordable option. It resists moisture reasonably well but chips easily. Best for rental properties or tight budgets in Fort Lauderdale.

**Thermofoil** is a vinyl wrap over MDF. It is seamless and easy to clean. It does not hold up well to high heat near ovens or dishwashers. Common in mid-2000s Florida homes.

**MDF Painted** offers a smooth finish without wood grain. It is stable and holds paint well. It swells if it gets wet, so avoid in wet areas like dishwashers.

**Plywood boxes** are the industry standard for durability. They resist warping better than particle board. Look for this in any mid-range to high-end quote.

**Solid wood** is the premium choice. It can be refinished multiple times. It expands and contracts with Florida humidity, requiring proper installation gaps.

**Custom built** cabinets are made to fit your exact space. They utilize every inch of storage. Ideal for older Fort Lauderdale homes with odd wall angles or low ceilings.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all interior remodels. If you move plumbing lines, you must use PEX or copper piping approved for potable water. Electrical work must meet NEC standards, especially for under-cabinet lighting.

**Moisture and Mold:** Fort Lauderdale has high humidity. Moisture can warp lower cabinets near sinks. Use marine-grade plywood or moisture-resistant MDF for base cabinets. Ensure proper ventilation under sinks to prevent mold growth.

**Permits:** Apply through the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Department online portal or at City Hall. Turnaround is 5–10 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$150–$400** for the fee. Inspections are required for plumbing and electrical changes.

**Insurance Impact:** Water damage claims are common in Florida. Upgrading to moisture-resistant cabinets can reduce the risk of claims. If a leak occurs, newer cabinets are easier to dry out and save from total replacement.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities restrict exterior cabinet colors or visible storage. If your kitchen opens to a living area, style matters. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement.

**Season:** Best time to remodel is December through April. Dry season means less humidity affecting wood installation. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October) when contractors are booked for storm repairs.

**Flood Zone:** If your home is in a coastal flood zone, avoid installing wooden cabinets below flood elevation. FEMA requires flood-resistant materials in designated zones. Check your flood map before ordering materials.

## How to Save Money on Cabinet Replacement in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Keep the existing layout.** Moving plumbing and gas lines is expensive. Keeping the sink and stove in the same spot saves **$1,000–$3,000** in plumbing labor and permit fees.

2. **Refinish instead of replace.** If boxes are solid but doors are dated, refinishing costs **$2,000–$4,000**. Replacing costs **$10,000+**. Painting existing cabinets is a viable option for budget homeowners.

3. **Order during the off-season.** December through March is slower for contractors. You might negotiate a **5–10% discount** on labor. Contractors are more available to start immediately without a long wait.

4. **Mix high and low cabinets.** Use wood for upper cabinets and laminate for lower ones. This balances durability with aesthetics. You save **$2,000–$5,000** on the total project cost.

5. **Handle demolition yourself.** If you are handy, tear out the old cabinets yourself. It saves **$500–$1,000** in labor. Just be careful not to damage walls or plumbing lines during removal.

## When to Replace Your Kitchen Cabinets

**Your cabinets are 15–20 years old.** Most cabinet boxes start to degrade after two decades. Drawer slides fail, hinges loosen, and doors sag. In Fort Lauderdale humidity, particle board boxes disintegrate faster than in dry climates.

**Water damage is visible.** Soft spots, swelling, or mold on the cabinet box indicate failure. If water has reached the subfloor, you must replace the cabinetry to prevent structural rot.

**Hardware is broken.** Soft-close mechanisms break and drawer slides rust. Replacing hardware is cheap, but if multiple drawers fail, the box structure is compromised.

**Style is outdated.** Open floor plans are popular in Fort Lauderdale. Closed-off kitchens with dated wood tones reduce home value. Modernizing the look can increase resale value by **$5,000–$10,000**.

**Storage is insufficient.** Older homes have shallow cabinets. Modern needs require deeper drawers and pull-outs. If you cannot fit your appliances or pantry items, a redesign is necessary.

## How to Hire a Cabinet Installer in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license.** Check with the Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR). You need a Certified or Registered Contractor for work over **$2,500**.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify coverage is current.

3. **Check for a local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered with the City of Fort Lauderdale, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, box construction, drawer slides, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Pay attention to comments about cleanup and timeline delays.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, material delivery, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a kitchen cabinet replacement cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$5,000 and $45,000**. The average is around **$15,000**. Your actual cost depends on material choice, kitchen size, and whether plumbing lines are moved.

### Do I need a permit to replace cabinets in Fort Lauderdale?

Yes, if you move plumbing or electrical. Apply through the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Department. Budget **$150–$400**. Two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a cabinet replacement take in Fort Lauderdale?

Stock cabinets take **3–5 days** for installation. Custom cabinets take **1–3 weeks** for fabrication plus **3–5 days** for install. Rainy season can delay deliveries or finish curing.

### Does replacing cabinets increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A kitchen remodel recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale’s market is marketability. Updated kitchens sell faster than homes with dated wood cabinets.

### Can I replace my own cabinets in Florida?

Florida allows homeowners to do their own work. But you still need permits for plumbing or electrical changes. If you make a mistake, you are liable for repairs. For most people, hiring a pro is safer.

### Does homeowners insurance cover cabinet replacement?

Storm damage or sudden water leaks are typically covered. Normal wear and tear or aging is never covered. Flood damage requires separate flood insurance.
