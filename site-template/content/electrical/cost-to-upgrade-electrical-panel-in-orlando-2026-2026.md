# How Much Does It Cost to Upgrade an Electrical Panel in Orlando? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A standard electrical panel upgrade in Orlando costs between **$2,000 and $5,500**, with the average homeowner paying around **$3,200**. The biggest factors driving your price are the amperage upgrade size, the brand of panel you choose, and Orange County permit fees.

Orlando homes built before 1980 often have 100-amp service that cannot handle modern air conditioning, EV chargers, or smart home technology. Upgrading to 200 amps is the most common fix. It ensures your home meets current code and keeps your insurance policy active.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$2,000 – $2,800** | 100A to 200A, existing conduit |
| Mid-Range | **$2,800 – $4,000** | 200A, new meter base, permit included |
| High-End | **$4,000 – $5,500+** | 200A+, service relocation, main breaker |

**Budget** covers a like-for-like swap where the main service line is already in place. It usually involves swapping a 100-amp panel for a 200-amp panel using existing conduit and meter locations.

**Mid-range** is what most Orlando homeowners need. It includes upgrading the meter base, new service entrance cable, and pulling the Orange County permit. This handles modern home loads comfortably.

**High-end** involves moving the service location, installing a 320-amp panel for large homes, or adding a whole-house generator interlock switch. This requires more labor and materials.

These prices reflect Orlando market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, permit fees, and utility disconnect costs. They do not include repairing damaged wiring found behind the panel during the upgrade.

## What Affects the Cost in Orlando

### 1. Amperage Upgrade Size

Most Orlando homes started with 100-amp service. Upgrading to 200 amps is the standard upgrade. This involves new service cables and a new main breaker. Going from 200 to 320 amps doubles the copper wire size and requires a larger meter socket. Expect to pay **$500–$1,500** more for the higher amperage upgrade.

### 2. Panel Brand and Quality

Square D, Siemens, and Eaton are the top three brands in Florida. A standard Square D QO panel costs **$200–$400**. Premium Siemens or Eaton load centers cost **$300–$600**. The price difference is real, but the performance gap is small for residential use. Ensure the panel is UL listed for Florida use.

### 3. Labor Rates in Orlando

Licensed master electricians in Orlando charge **$85–$120 per hour**. This is higher than the national average due to the high demand for skilled trades in Central Florida. A standard upgrade takes 4 to 8 hours. You are paying for the license to pull the permit, not just the labor.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Orange County Building Inspection Division requires a permit for any service upgrade. The permit fee runs **$150–$400** based on the project valuation. The process includes a plan review and a final inspection. Your electrician must schedule the inspection with the county. Never let a contractor skip the permit.

### 5. Utility Disconnect Fees

Orlando Utilities Commission (OUC) or Duke Energy Florida must disconnect power before the work begins. This fee ranges from **$150–$300**. OUC requires a 48-hour notice before they cut the power. You will need to coordinate this timing with your electrician to avoid double fees.

### 6. Wiring and Conduit Condition

If your existing conduit is corroded or damaged, you must replace it. This adds **$1,000–$3,000** to the project. Older homes in Orlando often have aluminum wiring that requires special connectors. If you need to rewire the feeder to the panel, costs climb significantly.

## Cost by Panel Type

| Panel Type | Cost Range | Amp Rating |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| 100A Standard | **$2,000 – $2,800** | 100 Amps |
| 200A Standard | **$2,800 – $4,000** | 200 Amps |
| 200A Premium | **$3,500 – $5,000** | 200 Amps |
| 320A Large Home | **$5,000 – $7,000+** | 320 Amps |
| Generator Interlock | **$1,500 – $2,500** | Add-on |
| Sub-Panel Add | **$1,200 – $2,000** | 60–100 Amps |

**100A Standard** panels are legacy units. They are rarely installed in new construction. You might find these in older Florida homes built in the 1970s. Upgrading away from them is highly recommended for safety.

**200A Standard** is the sweet spot for most single-family homes in Orlando. It supports central air, electric ranges, and dryers simultaneously without tripping. This covers the needs of a typical 3-bedroom home.

**200A Premium** panels include more breakers and higher quality bus bars. They often come with better warranties. The price jump is about **$500–$1,000** over the standard model.

**320A Large Home** is for estates or homes with electric vehicle charging stations and pool equipment. It requires heavier gauge wire and a larger meter base. This is a significant infrastructure investment.

**Generator Interlock** kits allow you to plug a portable generator into the panel safely. It costs **$1,500–$2,500** installed. This is a smart addition for Florida hurricane season power outages.

**Sub-Panel Add** is for homes with detached garages or workshops. It adds a new breaker box fed from the main. It costs **$1,200–$2,000** depending on distance from the main panel.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all electrical work in Orlando. Key requirements include specific grounding methods, AFCI protection in living areas, and GFCI protection in wet locations. Your electrician must use materials approved for Florida.

**HVHZ Requirements:** Orlando is not in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), which is limited to Miami-Dade and Broward. However, Orlando is still in a hurricane-prone zone. Outdoor disconnects and meter bases must be rated for wind loads and impact.

**Permits:** Apply through Orange County's online portal or at the Building Inspection Division. Turnaround is 3–5 business days for a standard residential permit. The final inspection must be passed before the utility company reconnects power.

**Insurance Impact:** Older panels like Federal Pacific Stab-Lok or Zinsko are often non-insurable. Upgrading to a modern panel improves your eligibility. Some insurers offer a **5–10% discount** for newer electrical systems that reduce fire risk.

**HOA:** Many Orlando communities restrict electrical work visibility. The meter base and disconnect must remain accessible. You cannot bury the new panel inside a closet without ventilation. Check your HOA covenants before starting work.

**Season:** Best time to upgrade is January through April. Electricians are less busy than during hurricane season. Rainy season (May–October) can delay utility disconnects if power lines are down.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection. While electrical upgrades don't always qualify for direct grants, they can be part of a larger mitigation package worth up to **$10,000**.

## How to Save Money on Electrical Panel Upgrades

1. **Bundle with other electrical work.** If you need new outlets or lighting, do it all at once. The electrician is already there with the equipment. You save on trip charges and permit fees, which are flat rates.

2. **Choose standard brands over custom.** Stick with Square D or Siemens standard lines. Custom panels or specialty brands add **$500–$1,000** to the bill without adding safety value for a standard home.

3. **Get 3 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–30%** in Orlando. On a **$3,000** job, that is **$600–$900** in potential savings. Ensure each quote includes the permit fee and utility coordination.

4. **Schedule in dry season.** December through April avoids the summer thunderstorm delays. Electricians can work faster when they aren't waiting for rain to clear. Some offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

5. **Check for insurance claim eligibility.** If your panel was damaged by a storm, your insurance may cover part of the replacement. File the claim before signing a contractor agreement. Be cautious of contractors who offer to "handle the insurance."

## When to Upgrade Your Electrical Panel — Warning Signs

**Your home is 20–30 years old.** Many Orlando homes from the 1990s have 100-amp service. Modern appliances draw more power than they did 30 years ago. An old panel is a fire risk and a load risk.

**Breakers trip frequently.** If you have to reset the breaker every time you turn on the AC, your panel is overloaded. This indicates the system cannot handle your current load. It is a safety hazard.

**Burning smell or discoloration.** Look at the breakers inside the panel. Brown marks on the breakers or a burning smell indicate overheating. This is an immediate fire risk. Turn off the main breaker and call a pro.

**Flickering lights.** Lights dimming when the washer kicks on suggest voltage drop. This strains your electronics and indicates a weak connection at the panel. It needs immediate attention.

**Insurance non-renewal.** If your insurer tells you the panel is a safety hazard, you must upgrade. Some companies will not renew policies on homes with Federal Pacific or Zinsko panels.

## How to Hire an Electrical Contractor in Orlando

1. **Verify their Florida license.** Search for a Certified Electrical Contractor (CEC) or Certified Master Electrician (CME) at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a state license, not just a county registration.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for Orange County registration.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state. They should know the OUC permit process.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — panel brand, wire gauge, permit fees, utility disconnect, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, materials delivered, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does an electrical panel upgrade cost in Orlando in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$2,000 and $5,500**. The average is around **$3,200**. Your actual cost depends on amperage, brand, and whether the service line needs replacement.

### Do I need a permit to upgrade my panel in Orange County?

Yes, always. Apply through Orange County's online portal. Budget **$150–$400**. Two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a panel upgrade take in Orlando?

The physical work takes **1 day** for a standard 200A upgrade. Utility disconnect and reconnection add 2–3 days for scheduling. Total project time is 3–5 days from start to power-on.

### Does a new panel increase home value in Orlando?

A new panel recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value is insurability. A home with a modern panel is easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

### Can I upgrade my own panel in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all FBC code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed panel. For most people, it is not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover panel replacement?

Storm damage (lightning strikes) is typically covered minus your deductible. Normal wear and aging is never covered. If the panel failed due to age, it is an out-of-pocket expense.
