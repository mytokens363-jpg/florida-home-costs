# How Much Does It Cost to Install a Metal Roof in Palm Beach? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full standing seam metal roof installation in Palm Beach costs between **$25,000 and $55,000** for most single-family homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$35,000**. The biggest factors driving your price are roof square footage, metal gauge thickness, and Palm Beach County’s strict coastal building requirements.

Palm Beach sits in a coastal zone with high wind exposure and salt air corrosion risks. That means your metal roof must meet specific Florida Building Code standards that inland areas often ignore. You pay a premium for durability, but you also secure a roof that handles storms and salt for decades.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$25,000 – $30,000** | Corrugated steel, 1,500 sq ft |
| Mid-Range | **$30,000 – $42,000** | Standing seam, 2,000 sq ft |
| High-End | **$42,000 – $55,000+** | Copper or Zinc, complex design |

**Budget** gets you exposed fastener corrugated steel on a simple roof. It meets code but uses thinner gauge metal and standard paint finishes. Best for guest houses or rental properties.

**Mid-range** is what most Palm Beach homeowners choose — 24-gauge or 26-gauge standing seam with concealed clips and Kynar 500 paint. Covers homes in the 2,000 to 3,000 sq ft range.

**High-end** covers copper, zinc, or aluminum standing seam on larger estates. Includes custom flashings, snow guards (rare but needed for ice dams), and premium warranties.

These prices reflect Palm Beach market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, tear-off and disposal, standard permits, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs if damage is found during tear-off.

## What Affects the Cost in Palm Beach

### 1. Roof Size

Metal roofing is priced per "square" (100 square feet). A typical Palm Beach home has a roof between 1,500 and 3,000 sq ft. At **$15.00–$25.00 per sq ft** installed depending on material, a 1,500 sq ft roof runs roughly **$25,000–$30,000** while a 3,000 sq ft roof pushes **$45,000–$60,000**.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Galvalume steel is the most common option at **$12.00–$18.00 per sq ft** installed. Aluminum — popular for coastal salt air — jumps to **$16.00–$24.00**. Standing seam copper is the premium choice at **$25.00–$40.00 per sq ft** but lasts 50–100 years.

### 3. Labor Rates in Palm Beach

Roofing labor in Palm Beach County runs **$60–$85 per hour**, roughly 20% above the national average. Demand stays high year-round from storm damage repairs and insurance-mandated replacements. After a named storm, rates can spike another 15–25%.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Palm Beach County requires a building permit for any metal roof installation. Permit fees run **$400–$900** depending on project valuation. The process includes a plan review and final inspection. Your contractor should pull the permit — if they suggest skipping it, walk away.

### 5. Roof Complexity

A simple gable roof is straightforward to replace. Add hips, valleys, dormers, or skylights and labor climbs. Complex roofs can add **$3,000–$8,000** to the project. Flat roofs — common on modern Palm Beach estates — use different metal systems at **$18.00–$28.00 per sq ft**.

### 6. Wind and Corrosion Codes

Palm Beach is in a Wind-Borne Debris Region. Every roofing product must meet Florida Building Code wind uplift standards. This limits material options and adds **$2,000–$5,000** compared to non-coastal areas. Fastener schedules are stricter too — concealed clip systems are often required for standing seam.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Corrugated Steel | **$12.00 – $16.00** | 20–30 years |
| Standing Seam Steel | **$16.00 – $22.00** | 30–50 years |
| Aluminum Standing Seam | **$18.00 – $26.00** | 40–60 years |
| Copper | **$25.00 – $40.00** | 50–100 years |
| Stone-Coated Metal | **$14.00 – $19.00** | 30–50 years |
| Zinc | **$22.00 – $35.00** | 60–100 years |

**Corrugated steel** is the budget option. Exposed fasteners are visible and prone to leaking over time. Best for agricultural buildings or budget-conscious homeowners.

**Standing seam steel** hits the sweet spot — concealed clips hide the fasteners, rated to 140 mph winds, and the best cost-to-value ratio. This is what most Palm Beach homeowners pick.

**Aluminum standing seam** is the classic coastal choice. Rated to 160 mph and resists salt corrosion naturally. It costs roughly 20% more than steel but lasts longer in beachfront zones.

**Stone-coated metal** offers the look of tile or shake with metal durability. Rated to 120 mph and lasts 30–50 years. It is heavier than standard metal and requires stronger framing.

**Copper** offers maximum durability at 50–100 years with 180 mph wind resistance. Best for historic Palm Beach homes where aesthetics matter. Higher upfront cost pays off over centuries.

**Zinc systems** are premium — 60–100 year lifespan, self-healing patina, and 160 mph rating. Expect to pay **$22.00–$35.00 per sq ft**. Rare but popular for luxury estates.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all roof replacements in Palm Beach. Key requirements include secondary water barrier on the entire roof deck, specific nail patterns for shingle attachment, and enhanced flashing at all penetrations. These go beyond what most states require.

**Hurricane Zone:** Palm Beach is in a Wind-Borne Debris Region, not the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ). All roofing materials must hold a Florida Product Approval. Products are tested with missile impact tests and cyclic pressure tests that simulate hurricane conditions. Your contractor should provide approval numbers for every product they plan to use.

**Permits:** Apply through Palm Beach County’s ePlan online portal or at the Building Division in West Palm Beach. Turnaround is 7–14 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$400–$900** for the fee. Two inspections required: pre-cover and final.

**Insurance Impact:** This is the big one. A new metal roof resets the clock with your insurance company. Many Florida insurers won't write or renew policies on homes with roofs older than 15 years — some draw the line at 10. Upgrading to a hip roof shape or adding hurricane straps during replacement can qualify you for wind mitigation discounts of **20–50%** on your premium.

**HOA:** Many Palm Beach communities restrict roofing material, color, and style. Tile or metal are often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement — changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

**Season:** Best time to replace a roof here is December through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm damage backlogs. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay projects.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants up to **$10,000** toward hurricane hardening improvements including roof upgrades.

## How to Save Money on Metal Roof Installation in Palm Beach

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Palm Beach. On a **$35,000** job, that's **$7,000–$12,000** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, tear-off, permits, and disposal separately.

2. **Schedule in dry season.** December through April is slower for roofers. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

3. **Choose standing seam steel over aluminum if your HOA allows it.** The savings between steel (**$16.00–$22.00/sq ft**) and aluminum (**$18.00–$26.00/sq ft**) can be **$3,000–$6,000** on a typical home.

4. **Bundle hurricane upgrades with the roof.** Adding roof-to-wall straps or upgraded underlayment during a replacement is **40–60% cheaper** than standalone projects. The combined wind mitigation improvements can also cut your insurance premium by hundreds per year.

5. **Check for insurance claim eligibility.** If your existing roof has storm damage, your insurance may cover part or all of the replacement. File the claim before signing a contractor agreement. Be cautious of contractors who offer to "handle the insurance" — work with your adjuster directly.

## When to Replace Your Roof — Warning Signs

**Your roof is 20–30 years old.** Metal roofs in South Florida degrade faster than in cooler climates due to intense UV, heat cycling, and salt air. A 25-year-old metal roof in Palm Beach has taken more abuse than a 40-year-old roof in the Midwest.

**Your insurance company is pressuring you.** A non-renewal notice or letter requesting a roof inspection means the clock is ticking. Many Florida insurers are actively dropping homes with aging roofs.

**Visible rust or corrosion on seams.** Check your gutters after heavy rain. Red dust or orange streaks on the siding mean the metal is oxidizing. This usually means the protective coating has failed.

**Interior water stains or attic moisture.** Brown ceiling spots, bubbling paint, or damp insulation point to roof leaks. In Palm Beach's humidity, even small leaks lead to mold fast. Multiple leaks usually mean the whole system has failed.

## How to Hire a Roofing Contractor in Palm Beach

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified Roofing Contractor (CCC), Registered Roofing Contractor (RC), or General Contractor (CGC) license.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Palm Beach County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, gauge thickness, underlayment type, number of squares, tear-off, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, tear-off complete, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with approval numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a metal roof installation cost in Palm Beach in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$25,000 and $55,000**. The average is around **$35,000**. Your actual cost depends on roof size, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to replace my roof in Palm Beach County?

Yes, always. Apply through Palm Beach County's ePlan portal or at the Building Division. Budget **$400–$900**. Two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a metal roof replacement take in Palm Beach?

Standing seam roofs take **5–10 days** for an average home. Aluminum or copper can take **10–15 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays.

### Does a new metal roof increase home value in Palm Beach?

A new metal roof recoups **65–75%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Palm Beach's market is insurability — a home with a new metal roof is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

### Can I replace my own roof in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all Florida Building Code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed roof. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover metal roof replacement?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.
