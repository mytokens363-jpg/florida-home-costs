# How Much Does It Cost to Install Impact Doors in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Installing impact doors in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$3,500 and $15,000** for most projects, with the average homeowner paying around **$7,500**. The final price depends heavily on the number of doors, material selection, and whether structural framing changes are required.

Fort Lauderdale sits inside the High Velocity Hurricane Zone, which mandates strict testing standards for all exterior doors. This requirement adds cost compared to standard non-impact doors, but it provides critical protection against wind-driven debris and storm surge.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$3,500 – $5,500** | Single entry door, standard size |
| Mid-Range | **$5,500 – $9,000** | Entry + 1 patio door, fiberglass |
| High-End | **$9,000 – $15,000+** | Multiple doors, custom wood, glass |

**Budget** covers a single entry door replacement using standard fiberglass or steel with pre-hung units. It assumes the existing rough opening fits the new door without major carpentry work.

**Mid-range** typically includes a front entry door plus one sliding patio door or French door. Materials are usually fiberglass or aluminum-clad wood. Includes standard hardware and trim.

**High-end** involves multiple impact-rated doors, custom wood finishes, or large glass expanses. Requires structural reinforcement, new framing, and potentially electrical work for integrated lighting or smart locks.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, disposal, standard permits, and cleanup. They do not include major structural repairs if rot or water damage is found behind the existing frame.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Door Type and Quantity

A single front entry door is the baseline. Adding a sliding glass patio door or a set of French doors increases the linear footage of work significantly. A sliding door requires a wider opening and a heavier frame to resist wind pressure. Installing three doors instead of one can easily double the total project cost.

### 2. Material Selection

Fiberglass is the most popular choice at **$1,500–$3,000 per door** installed. It offers good insulation and mimics wood grain. Steel doors are slightly cheaper at **$1,200–$2,500** but dent easier. Wood doors are premium, ranging from **$3,000–$6,000+** per door. They require more maintenance but offer the highest aesthetic value.

### 3. Frame and Rough Opening

If the existing opening is damaged or not square, the contractor must rebuild the rough opening. This involves cutting into the stucco, adjusting concrete block headers, and replacing framing lumber. Framing repairs can add **$1,000–$3,000** to the project depending on the extent of the damage.

### 4. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Specialized door installers in Broward County charge **$65–$95 per hour**. This is higher than general carpentry due to the precision required for impact-rated systems. Complex installations involving glass panes over 50 inches require two-person crews to prevent breakage during installation.

### 5. Permits and Inspections

Broward County requires a building permit for any exterior door replacement that involves structural changes or new openings. Permit fees run **$100–$300** depending on the project valuation. The process includes a rough inspection before the frame is covered and a final inspection after installation.

### 6. HVHZ Code Requirements

Fort Lauderdale is in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone. Every door must carry a Miami-Dade NOA or Florida Product Approval. This testing ensures the unit can withstand 150 mph to 180 mph winds and missile impact. Non-compliant doors are illegal to install and will fail inspection.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Door | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Fiberglass | **$1,500 – $3,000** | 20–30 years |
| Steel | **$1,200 – $2,500** | 15–25 years |
| Aluminum | **$2,000 – $4,000** | 25–40 years |
| Wood | **$3,000 – $6,000+** | 30+ years |
| Sliding Glass | **$3,500 – $7,000** | 20–30 years |

**Fiberglass** is the standard recommendation for most Fort Lauderdale homeowners. It resists humidity better than wood and does not corrode like aluminum. The material also provides better thermal insulation, which helps lower cooling bills during the summer months.

**Steel** is the most affordable option. It is strong and secure but can dent if hit by large debris. Modern steel doors have foam cores that provide decent insulation. Paint finishes on steel require touch-ups to prevent rust in coastal areas.

**Aluminum** is common for sliding patio doors. It is lightweight and resistant to rust but conducts heat faster than fiberglass. Thermal break aluminum frames are available to improve energy efficiency. Expect to pay more for the thermal break feature.

**Wood** offers a classic look that fits historic neighborhoods in Fort Lauderdale. However, wood absorbs moisture and requires regular sealing to prevent warping. The installation is more delicate to ensure the seal remains tight against wind and rain.

**Sliding Glass** doors are larger and require heavier framing. The glass must be laminated and impact-rated to meet HVHZ standards. The track system needs to be heavy-duty to handle daily use and wind pressure.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all exterior door installations in Fort Lauderdale. Key requirements include secondary water barriers, specific anchoring schedules for the frame, and reinforced glass. These go beyond what most states require for standard residential work.

**Hurricane Zone:** Fort Lauderdale is in the HVHZ — the strictest wind zone in Florida. All impact products must hold a Miami-Dade NOA. Products are tested with missile impact tests and cyclic pressure tests that simulate hurricane conditions. Your contractor should provide NOA numbers for every product they plan to use.

**Permits:** Apply through Broward County's Building Division online portal or at the main facility in Fort Lauderdale. Turnaround is 3–7 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$100–$300** for the fee. One inspection required after installation to verify anchoring and flashing.

**Insurance Impact:** This is the big one. Impact doors qualify for wind mitigation discounts. Many Florida insurers offer a reduction of **10–30%** on the wind portion of your premium for impact-rated entry and patio doors. Verify the discount before signing a contract.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities restrict door color, style, and hardware finish. Wood grain textures are often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement — changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

**Season:** Best time to install doors here is November through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm backlogs. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay projects and can damage exposed framing.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants up to **$10,000** toward hurricane hardening improvements including door upgrades.

## How to Save Money on Impact Door Installation in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$7,500** job, that's **$1,500–$2,500** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, framing, permits, and disposal separately.

2. **Schedule in dry season.** November through April is slower for door installers. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts to keep crews busy.

3. **Choose fiberglass over wood if your HOA allows it.** The savings between fiberglass (**$1,500–$3,000**) and custom wood (**$3,000–$6,000**) can be **$1,500–$3,000** per door. Fiberglass also requires less maintenance in Florida's humid climate.

4. **Bundle hurricane upgrades with the roof.** Adding roof-to-wall straps or upgraded underlayment during a roof replacement is **40–60% cheaper** than standalone projects. The combined wind mitigation improvements can also cut your insurance premium by hundreds per year.

5. **Check for insurance claim eligibility.** If your existing doors are damaged by wind or storm, your insurance may cover part or all of the replacement. File the claim before signing a contractor agreement. Be cautious of contractors who offer to "handle the insurance" — work with your adjuster directly.

## When to Replace Your Doors — Warning Signs

**Your doors are 15–20 years old.** Impact technology has improved significantly since the early 2000s. Older impact doors may not meet current HVHZ standards. Newer models offer better thermal performance and wind resistance ratings.

**Your insurance company is pressuring you.** A non-renewal notice or letter requesting a door inspection means the clock is ticking. Many Florida insurers are actively dropping homes with aging exterior openings that are not impact-rated.

**Visible cracks in the frame.** Check the stucco around the door frame for hairline cracks. These indicate movement from wind or settling. Cracks let water in, leading to rot in the surrounding wood or concrete block.

**Difficulty opening or closing the door.** If the door drags or sticks, the frame may be warped. Warped frames break the weather seal, allowing wind and rain to penetrate during a storm. This compromises the integrity of the impact rating.

## How to Hire an Impact Door Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified Roofing Contractor (CCC), Registered Roofing Contractor (RC), or General Contractor (CGC) license.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, underlayment type, number of squares, tear-off, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, rough-in complete, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does impact door installation cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$3,500 and $15,000**. The average is around **$7,500**. Your actual cost depends on door count, material choice, complexity, and whether structural framing is needed.

### Do I need a permit to replace impact doors in Broward County?

Yes, always. Apply through Broward County's Building Division. Budget **$100–$300**. One inspection required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does impact door installation take in Fort Lauderdale?

Standard replacement takes **1–2 days** for a single door. Multiple doors or sliding units take **2–4 days**. Rainy season can add 1–2 days of weather delays.

### Does a new impact door increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

Impact doors recoup **70–80%** of their cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is insurability — a home with impact doors is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

### Can I replace my own door in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all HVHZ code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed door. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover impact door replacement?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.
