# How Much Does It Cost to Install a Hurricane Garage Door in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full hurricane-rated garage door installation in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$4,500 and $9,000** for most double-car garages, with the average homeowner paying around **$6,750**. The biggest factors driving your price are door size, material choice, and Broward County's strict high-velocity hurricane zone requirements.

Fort Lauderdale sits inside the High Velocity Hurricane Zone, which means your garage door has to meet standards that most of the country does not deal with. That adds cost, but it also means your door is built to handle what Florida throws at it.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$4,500 – $5,500** | Single steel, non-insulated |
| Mid-Range | **$5,500 – $7,500** | Double steel, insulated |
| High-End | **$7,500 – $9,000+** | Custom wood, smart opener |

**Budget** gets you a single-car impact-rated steel door with basic wind bracing and a standard opener. It meets code but uses entry-level insulation and finish.

**Mid-range** is what most Fort Lauderdale homeowners choose — double-car doors rated for 170 mph winds, polyurethane insulation, and synthetic underlayment. Covers the majority of 2-car garage sizes.

**High-end** covers custom wood-composite or premium aluminum designs on larger openings. Includes enhanced wind bracing, smart home integration, and upgraded tracks for smoother operation.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, removal of old door, disposal, standard permits, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs if the frame is damaged during installation.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Garage Door Size

Garage doors are priced by width and height. A standard single door is **8 or 9 feet wide**. A standard double door is **16 feet wide**. At **$250–$500 per square foot** installed depending on material, an 8-foot door runs roughly **$3,500–$5,000** while a 16-foot door pushes **$6,000–$9,000**.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Steel is the most common option at **$1,500–$2,500 per door** installed. Wood composite — popular in South Florida — jumps to **$3,000–$5,000**. Aluminum is the premium choice for coastal homes at **$2,500–$4,000** but requires more maintenance.

### 3. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Garage door labor in Broward County runs **$80–$120 per hour**, roughly 10–15% above the national average. Demand stays high year-round from storm damage repairs and insurance-mandated upgrades. After a named storm, rates can spike another 10–20%.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Broward County and the City of Fort Lauderdale require a building permit for any garage door replacement in HVHZ areas. Permit fees run **$150–$400** depending on project value. The process includes a pre-installation check and final inspection. Your contractor should pull the permit.

### 5. Opener Integration

A new impact door often requires a new opener to match the wind rating and weight. Smart openers with Wi-Fi and battery backups cost extra. A standard impact opener adds **$400–$800** to the total project cost.

### 6. HVHZ Code Requirements

Fort Lauderdale is inside the High Velocity Hurricane Zone. Every garage door product must carry a Miami-Dade NOA or Florida Product Approval. This limits material options and adds **$1,000–$2,500** compared to non-HVHZ areas. Bracing schedules are stricter too.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all garage door replacements in Fort Lauderdale. Key requirements include specific wind pressure ratings, positive pressure testing, and reinforced anchoring systems. These go beyond what most states require.

**Hurricane Zone:** Fort Lauderdale is in the HVHZ — the strictest wind zone in Florida. All garage door products must hold a Miami-Dade NOA. Products are tested with missile impact tests and cyclic pressure tests that simulate hurricane conditions. Your contractor should provide NOA numbers for every product they plan to use.

**Permits:** Apply through the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Division online portal or at City Hall. Turnaround is 5–10 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$150–$400** for the fee. One inspection required: final installation.

**Insurance Impact:** This is the big one. A new impact-rated door resets your wind mitigation status. Many Florida insurers offer credits for impact-rated garage doors. Upgrading to an impact door can qualify you for wind mitigation discounts of **10–20%** on your premium.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities restrict door color, style, and window inserts. Wood grain finishes are often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement.

**Season:** Best time to install a garage door here is December through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm damage backlogs. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay projects.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants up to **$10,000** toward hurricane hardening improvements including garage door upgrades.

## How to Save Money on Garage Door Installation in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$7,000** job, that's **$1,500–$2,500** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, disposal, permits, and hardware separately.

2.  **Schedule in dry season.** December through April is slower for garage door installers. You will have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

3.  **Choose steel over wood if your HOA allows it.** The savings between steel (**$5,500–$7,500**) and wood composite (**$6,000–$9,000**) can be **$1,000–$2,000** on a typical double-car installation.

4.  **Bundle garage upgrades with the roof.** Adding garage door strapping or upgraded tracks during a replacement is **40–60% cheaper** than standalone projects. The combined wind mitigation improvements can also cut your insurance premium by hundreds per year.

5.  **Check for insurance claim eligibility.** If your existing door has storm damage, your insurance may cover part or all of the replacement. File the claim before signing a contractor agreement. Be cautious of contractors who offer to "handle the insurance."

## When to Install a Hurricane Garage Door — Warning Signs

**Your current door is not rated.** Check the inside of the door panel for a label. If it does not list a Miami-Dade NOA or Florida Product Approval number, it is not hurricane-rated. This leaves your home vulnerable to catastrophic failure during a storm.

**Your insurance company is pressuring you.** A non-renewal notice or letter requesting a garage inspection means the clock is ticking. Many Florida insurers are actively dropping homes with non-impact-rated garage doors in coastal zones.

**Visible damage to the frame.** Look for rust, bending, or cracks in the tracks and frame. In Fort Lauderdale's salt air, corrosion happens fast. Structural failure of the frame means the door cannot hold pressure.

**Interior water stains or attic moisture.** Water entering through the garage door gap or through the door itself points to system failure. In Fort Lauderdale's humidity, even small leaks lead to mold fast. Multiple leaks usually mean the whole system has failed.

## How to Hire a Garage Door Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC), Certified Building Contractor (CBC), or Garage Door Specialist license.

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3.  **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, wind rating, number of panels, removal, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6.  **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, door delivery, installation complete, final inspection passed.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a hurricane garage door installation cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$4,500 and $9,000**. The average is around **$6,750**. Your actual cost depends on door size, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to replace my garage door in Broward County?

Yes, always. Apply through the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Division portal. Budget **$150–$400**. One inspection required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a garage door installation take in Fort Lauderdale?

Standard steel doors take **4–6 hours** for an average installation. Wood doors take **1–2 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays for structural work.

### Does a new garage door increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A new garage door recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is insurability — a home with a new impact door is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

### Can I install my own garage door in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all HVHZ code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed door. For most people, it is not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover garage door replacement?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.
